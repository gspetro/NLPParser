Product Identifier 

Cigarette Ignition Strength Standard  

SRM Number:  1082 
SRM Name: 
 
Under the U.S. Department of Labor, Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) 29 CFR 1910.1200, 
Safety  Data  Sheet  information  is  not  required.    This  document  may  be  used  in  conjunction  with  your  hazard 
communication program. 
 
Exemption:  1910.1200(b)(6)(iii).  Tobacco and tobacco products. 
 
Description:  This Standard Reference Material (SRM) is intended for use by test laboratories to assess and control 
their testing of cigarette ignition strength in accordance with ASTM Standard Methods E2187-16 (and earlier versions 
of this standard) and with the nearly identical ISO 12863.  A unit of SRM 1082 consists of one carton of cigarettes 
containing 10 packs of 20 cigarettes each and is shipped on dry ice. 
 
Additional  Notes:    These  cigarettes  are  NOT  FOR  HUMAN  CONSUMPTION.    There  are  substantial  safety 
hazards associated with exposure to both primary and second hand smoke from cigarettes.  These cigarettes are only 
to be used under laboratory conditions described in ASTM E2187-16.  
 
Disposal:  SRM 1082 components should be disposed of in accordance with local, state, and federal regulations. 
 
Transport  Information:    This  material  is  not  regulated  by  the  U.S.  Department  of  Transportation (DOT)  and/or 
International Air Transport Association (IATA).
